Why MCP-First

What MCP-first actually means

Most note apps were built before AI mattered. Legate Studio was built after — with AI integration as the primary interface, not an add-on.

MCP (Model Context Protocol) is the open standard that lets AI assistants reach outside their own context window and query external tools and data sources. When your AI has an MCP connection to your knowledge base, it's not working from memory or generic training data — it's working from your actual notes, in real time.

Most personal knowledge management tools treat AI as a feature layered on top: a chat window in the sidebar, a summarize button, an AI-powered search. MCP-first means the opposite. The MCP interface is the primary way the knowledge base gets used. The web app is where you capture and organize; the MCP server is where AI gets work done with your knowledge.

Practically: you connect Legate Studio to Claude Desktop by adding one config entry. From that point, your AI assistant can search your knowledge base, retrieve specific notes by category or keyword, and create new entries — all from inside the AI conversation, without you doing anything manually.

This changes what AI is useful for. An AI that knows your context — your projects, your decisions, your terminology — is a different tool than one that's starting from zero every session.

Ownership & Privacy

Your data, your control

We built Legate Studio around a simple principle: your knowledge is yours. Not in a marketing-copy sense — in a technical, architectural sense.

GitHub-Backed Storage

Your knowledge lives in a GitHub repository you own. Full version history, portable, exportable. Clone it anytime — no export process, no proprietary format. Your notes are Markdown files.

Private by Architecture

We don't read your notes, train on your content, or store your knowledge beyond what's required to run the app. Your GitHub repo is private by default. We can't access it even if we wanted to.

No Lock-In

Because your data is in a GitHub repo you control, leaving Legate Studio means nothing is lost. Clone the repo, delete the app — your knowledge is still there, readable by any Markdown editor.
